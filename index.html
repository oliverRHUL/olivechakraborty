// About me
Who I am

I am a cryptographer and research scientist at CEA LIST in Palaiseau, France, with over 8 years of experience in post-quantum cryptography and fully homomorphic encryption. My work sits at the intersection of deep mathematical theory and engineering reality — I design new cryptographic primitives, break existing ones, and build systems that protect sensitive data even when it's being computed on.

I completed my PhD at team PolSys, Sorbonne Université (erstwhile Pierre and Marie Curie University, Paris 6), under the supervision of Jean-Charles Faugère and Ludovic Perret. Before that, I completed an M.Sc. in Information Security at Royal Holloway, University of London, where I worked under the supervision of Martin Albrecht for my dissertation. I completed my undergraduate degree at BITS Pilani, India on Computer Science and Mathematics. I was also very fortunate to do a research internship at Team GAMBLE (ex VEGAS) INRIA Nancy, working with Marc Pouget on certified algorithms for algebraic geometry.

I am interested in all aspects of fully homomorphic encryption, multi-party computation and more broadly, computing on encrypted data, privacy-preserving machine learning and post-quantum cryptography

Beyond theory, I lead EU-funded research projects on privacy-preserving identity verification and secure biometric systems (SafeTravellers, Prividema), supervise PhD and Master's students, and teach Advanced Cryptography at Télécom Paris.

// Research
Active Research Areas

My work spans theoretical foundations and practical implementations of next-generation cryptographic systems.

🔐
Post-Quantum Cryptography
Designing and analyzing cryptographic schemes based on hard lattice problems (LWE, SIS) and multivariate problems (MQ) that resist attacks by quantum computers. Contributing to NIST PQC standardization.
Lattice-based · Multivariate
🧮
Fully Homomorphic Encryption
Advancing the efficiency of FHE schemes (CKKS, BFV, TFHE) across a range of applications — from privacy-preserving machine learning and federated learning aggregation, to secure speaker recognition, oblivious processing, and ciphertext compression. Contributions include multi-key FHE protocols, homomorphic comparisons, and lookup table evaluation over encrypted data.
BFV · BGV · TFHE · CKKS
∞
Algebraic Foundations
Studying the algebraic structures underlying modern cryptography — including ideal lattices, number fields, and polynomial rings — to strengthen hardness assumptions.
Ring-LWE · Algebraic NT
🔍
Zero-Knowledge Proofs
Developing efficient ZK proof systems that are post-quantum secure, enabling verifiable computation without revealing private inputs.
ZK · Sigma Protocols
⚡
Secure Multi-Party Computation
Building MPC protocols on top of PQC and FHE primitives for collaborative computation where no single party holds the full secret.
MPC · Secret Sharing
📐
Cryptanalysis
Analyzing the concrete security of lattice problems using BKZ-style algorithms and estimating parameters for real-world deployments.
BKZ · LWE Estimator
